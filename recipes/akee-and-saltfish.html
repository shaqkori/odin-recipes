Akee and salt fish

Ingreedients:

  • 1 can (19 ounces) of ackee, drained and rinsed (fresh ackee can also be used if available)
  • 8 ounces of salted codfish (saltfish)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1/2 bell pepper (red or green), chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 scallions, chopped
  • Scotch bonnet pepper (or habanero pepper), finely chopped (adjust to your preferred level of spiciness)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme (or 2 sprigs fresh thyme)
  • 1/2 teaspoon allspice (pimento) berries (optional)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish (optional)
  1. Prepare the Saltfish: Rinse the salted codfish under cold running water to remove excess salt. Cut the codfish into smaller pieces. In a medium-sized pot, add the codfish pieces and cover them with cold water. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 15-20 minutes to soften the fish. Taste a small piece to check for saltiness. Drain the codfish and rinse it again to remove any remaining salt. Shred the codfish into small flakes and set it aside.
  2. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large skillet or frying pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions, bell pepper, and garlic. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until the onions become translucent.
  3. Combine Ingredients: Add the shredded saltfish to the skillet with the sautéed vegetables. Stir in the chopped tomatoes, scallions, Scotch bonnet pepper, black pepper, dried thyme, and allspice (if using). Continue to cook and stir for another 2-3 minutes until everything is well combined.
  4. Add Ackee : Gently fold in the drained ackee, being careful not to break the ackee into small pieces. You want to maintain the ackee's texture. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, gently stirring to heat the ackee thoroughly.
  5. Season and Serve: Taste the dish and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Be cautious with the salt, as the saltfish is already salty. Remove any whole pimento berries if you used them. Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro, if desired.
Ackee and Saltfish is traditionally served with fried dumplings, boiled green bananas, or rice. It's a delicious and flavorful dish with a unique combination of flavors. Enjoy!
